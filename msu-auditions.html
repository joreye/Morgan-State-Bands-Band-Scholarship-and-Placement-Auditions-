Music Requirements for All Auditions

1) Scales — Required (Standard for ALL Auditions)

  • Chromatic scale: full practical range; steady tempo; even tone.
  • Major scales: all 12; two octaves when possible (use the most practical range if two octaves are not possible).
  • Minor scales: all minor scales in Natural / Harmonic / Melodic forms; two octaves when possible.
  • Prepare as marked on the included scale sheets.
  • Memorization: preferred (can strengthen scholarship/competitive placement), not required.
Scholarship Benchmarks

For strongest scholarship consideration, complete the full scale standard and demonstrate accurate tone, time, and intonation.

2) Performance Material — Excerpts/Selections

  • Required: at least two contrasting selections or the assigned excerpts for your instrument.
  • Examples of contrast: lyrical vs. technical; slow vs. fast; legato vs. articulated; different styles/eras.
  • Top consideration: perform all assigned excerpts and complete the full scale requirements.
Audition Outcomes (One Audition, Multiple Uses)

Scholarship consideration, major/non-major placement, ensemble placement, and part placement.

Evaluation Criteria

Tone quality, intonation, rhythmic accuracy/time feel, technique & articulation clarity, range & endurance, musicality, preparation & professionalism.

Your Built-In “Cheat Code” (Use It.)

Every excerpt page includes a blue hyperlink to a professional performance and interpretation of that exact excerpt. Use it to lock in tempo, style, articulation, tone, and phrasing—and to reduce audition stress. It’s a reference tool, not a substitute for practice.

Quick Checklist

  • Scales: chromatic + all major + all minor (Nat/Harm/Melodic), 2 octaves when possible.
  • Excerpts: play the assigned excerpts; add contrasting material if needed.
  • Scholarship goal: play all excerpts + complete the full scale standard.
  • Video: single continuous takes, no edits, no enhancers.
  • Live: disable noise cancellation / use music-friendly audio settings.

Recorded Video Submission Standards

  • Single continuous take per selection/excerpt (no splicing/edits).
  • Camera shows face and instrument; hands clearly visible.
  • Quiet space, minimal echo (avoid fans/HVAC when possible).
  • No reverb, compression, EQ, Dolby, “voice focus,” or enhancement filters.
  • At the start of each video, state: Name · Instrument · Pathway (Scholarship / Major / Non-Major / Ensemble / Part Placement).
  • Do a 10-second test of your loudest passage to confirm it is not distorted or “pumping.”

Placement tip: device 6–10 feet away, slightly off-axis, to avoid harsh tone and distortion.

Live Audition Audio Settings (Zoom / Google Meet)

Goal: prevent the app from treating your instrument as background noise and cutting it out.

Zoom (music-friendly settings)
  • Desktop: Zoom → Settings → Audio → turn ON Original Sound for Musicians (or “Original Sound”).
  • Set background noise suppression to Low or Off.
  • Turn OFF: Automatically adjust microphone volume (if shown).
  • In the meeting, confirm Original Sound is toggled ON.
  • Mobile: enable “Original Sound/Original Audio” if available; otherwise use desktop when possible.
Google Meet (best settings)
  • Desktop (Chrome recommended): Meet → Settings (gear) → Audio → select correct mic.
  • Turn OFF: Noise cancellation (critical for instruments).
  • Set mic level so it does not clip (avoid red/peaking).
  • Mobile: if you can’t find noise cancellation settings, use a laptop/desktop.
iPhone / iPad mic modes (important)
  • Disable “voice enhancement,” “noise reduction,” “Dolby,” or similar processing.
  • During the call: set Mic Mode to Standard (avoid Voice Isolation; Wide Spectrum may help if available).
  • Use Wi-Fi when possible; avoid Bluetooth earbuds for instrumental auditions.

Sight-Reading / Musicianship (If Used)

  • Sight-reading may be required for ensemble placement and/or part placement.
  • If included, students may receive a brief silent study period before performing (commonly 30 seconds).
  • Materials may include mixed meters, articulations, style markings, and dynamic contrasts.
  • Percussion (priority scholarship consideration): may be asked to sight-read on snare and mallets.

Jazz Audition Addendum (If Auditioning for Jazz Ensemble)

If you are auditioning for Jazz Ensemble/Jazz Band placement using the jazz packet, prepare the following:

  • Assigned jazz etude(s)/excerpts (as provided).
  • Improvisation (swing style): Bb Blues (two 12-bar choruses at 128 bpm) and Rhythm Changes (one 32-bar chorus at 180 bpm).
  • Follow packet instructions for tuning tones and count-offs.
  • Sight-reading may be included depending on ensemble needs.

Note: If auditioning for both concert band placement and jazz placement, submit required materials for each pathway.

FAQ

Do I have to memorize scales?

Memorization is preferred but not required. Memorization and full completion of the scale standard can strengthen scholarship and competitive placement outcomes.

How many excerpts should I play?

Prepare at least two contrasting selections or the assigned excerpts. For top consideration, perform all assigned excerpts and complete the full scale requirements.

Can I submit recorded auditions and also do a live audition?

Yes—when offered. Recorded submissions must be continuous takes with no edits. Live auditions should use the music-friendly audio settings on this page.

Where do I get the excerpt packet and scale sheets?

Use the official packet and scale sheets provided by Morgan State University Bands.
